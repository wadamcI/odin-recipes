MASA-D Recipes

Every year, the team travels to one of the southern states in America to compete in the International Rocket Engineering Competition (IREC), consisting of approximately 30 participants with varying tastes, allergies, religions, and other dietary preferences or restrictions.

The competition lasts five days. While the first one or two are spent inside a temperature-controlled environment, the latter part takes place predominantly in the desert.

As the culmination of a year’s work, the competition serves as a prime team-building exercise. The challenges strengthen bonds, victories are celebrated together, and failures foster a determination for improvement that uplifts team spirit.

Therefore, nutrition becomes a task of utmost importance. It must not only ease competitors into heat acclimatization and fuel the exhaustion of trekking through the desert, but also provide the comfort of a warm mother’s embrace after the stresses of the environment.

It is with the core belief that a bad day can be saved by a good meal, but not the other way around, that this ever-evolving repository of recipes and methods is written: as a guide to a successful competition.

Ideals

  1. A good meal can save a bad day — but a bad meal will never save a good one.
  2. Hunger is the finest seasoning.
  3. The cook’s patience is essential; the crowd’s impatience is a ticking time bomb.
  4. A recipe is a suggestion; tasting is the only way to be sure.
  5. Given enough prep time, even Batman can beat anyone.
  6. If it’s edible and on time, you’ve already won.
